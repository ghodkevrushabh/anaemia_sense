What is Anaemia?

Anaemia is a medical condition characterized by a deficiency in the number or quality of red blood cells in the body. Red blood cells contain hemoglobin, a protein that carries oxygen from the lungs to all parts of the body.

When you have anaemia, your body doesn't get enough oxygen-rich blood, leading to feelings of tiredness and weakness.

Types of Anaemia

  • Iron-deficiency anaemia: Most common type, caused by insufficient iron
  • Vitamin-deficiency anaemia: Lack of vitamin B12 or folate
  • Aplastic anaemia: Body stops producing enough red blood cells
  • Hemolytic anaemia: Red blood cells are destroyed faster than they can be replaced
  • Sickle cell anaemia: Inherited condition affecting hemoglobin shape

Common Symptoms

  • Persistent fatigue and weakness
  • Pale or yellowish skin
  • Irregular heartbeat
  • Shortness of breath
  • Dizziness or lightheadedness
  • Cold hands and feet
  • Headaches
  • Chest pain

Risk Factors

  • Poor diet lacking in iron, vitamin B12, or folate
  • Intestinal disorders affecting nutrient absorption
  • Menstruation (heavy periods)
  • Pregnancy
  • Chronic conditions (kidney disease, cancer, etc.)
  • Family history of inherited anemia
  • Age over 65

Treatment Options

Treatment depends on the type and cause of anaemia:

  • Iron supplements: For iron-deficiency anaemia
  • Vitamin supplements: B12 or folate supplements
  • Dietary changes: Iron-rich foods, leafy greens
  • Medications: To boost red blood cell production
  • Blood transfusions: For severe cases
  • Treating underlying conditions: Addressing root causes

Prevention Tips

  • Eat iron-rich foods: red meat, beans, leafy greens
  • Include vitamin C to enhance iron absorption
  • Consume foods rich in folate and vitamin B12
  • Avoid excessive tea or coffee with meals
  • Regular health check-ups and blood tests
  • Manage chronic conditions properly
